About Me

My name is Nyagilo Stanley, and my journey into technology began in a small rural village called Kigoto in Kenya. I was born on 10th September 2003 and raised near the shores of Lake Victoria, the largest lake in Africa. Growing up in a lakeside community shaped my early life and taught me resilience, discipline, and the importance of hard work.

Early Life Near Lake Victoria

Life near Lake Victoria was simple and deeply connected to nature and community. Many families depended on fishing and small-scale farming. Resources were limited but community bonds were strong. Electricity, computers, and internet access were scarce. Despite this, I developed curiosity about technology whenever I saw or heard about computers. I often wondered how machines worked and how technology shaped the world beyond our village.

Early Education

I began my formal education at Kigoto Primary School until 2017 when I completed my KCPE. School shaped my ambitions and exposed me to new possibilities. Although access to computers was minimal, my interest in technology grew. I was fascinated by how computers could connect people, store information, and solve complex problems.

Secondary School Journey

In 2018, I joined Sori Boys High School. My interest in computers and technology became stronger. I became curious about how digital systems function and solve real-world problems. I successfully completed my KCSE in 2021.

University and Discovering Cybersecurity

After high school, I joined Masinde Muliro University of Science and Technology (MMUST) to pursue Information Technology and Information Management. I was exposed to networking, programming, databases, and information systems. One field captured my attention more than others: Cybersecurity. I actively participated in workshops, learning communities, and mentoring students in cybersecurity awareness.

My Passion for Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity is not just an academic field for me; it is a mission. My goal is to develop strong expertise in cybersecurity, ethical hacking, and digital defense, helping organizations stay safe and mentoring young students.

Looking Forward

My journey from a small village near Lake Victoria to studying technology at university taught me that determination, curiosity, and education can transform lives. I want to use my skills to contribute to the tech industry and inspire others from villages like Kigoto to pursue technology careers.

Conclusion

Every journey starts somewhere. Mine began near the waters of Lake Victoria in Kigoto village. Today, it continues in the world of Information Technology and Cybersecurity, where I am committed to learning, protecting digital systems, and making a positive impact through technology.
